Bhangarh Fort

About:

Bhangarh Fort, located in the Alwar district of Rajasthan, India, is infamous for its eerie reputation as one of the most haunted places in the country. Built in the 17th century by Raja Madho Singh, the fort stands as a testament to Rajput architecture with its grandiose gates, temples, palaces, and havelis. Legend has it that the fort was cursed by a sorcerer, leading to its abandonment and subsequent haunting. Despite its haunting reputation, Bhangarh Fort attracts curious visitors fascinated by its mysterious aura and picturesque surroundings. The Archaeological Survey of India has placed restrictions on visiting the fort after sunset, adding to its mystique. Beyond its paranormal associations, Bhangarh Fort offers a glimpse into Rajasthan's rich history and architectural heritage, with its crumbling walls and intricately carved stone structures serving as silent witnesses to centuries of tumultuous history.
