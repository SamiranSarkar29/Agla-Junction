Kalighat Kali Temple



About:

Kalighat Kali Temple, situated in Kolkata, West Bengal, is one of the most revered Hindu temples dedicated to Goddess Kali. The temple's origins are ancient, with references dating back to the 15th century. According to legend, it is believed to be one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, where a part of Goddess Sati's body fell during Lord Shiva's Tandava dance. The temple attracts millions of devotees and pilgrims each year who come to seek blessings from the fierce yet compassionate form of Goddess Kali. The architecture of the temple is simple yet striking, with the main sanctum housing a black stone idol of Goddess Kali adorned with gold ornaments and flowers. The atmosphere around the temple is charged with spirituality, and visitors can witness various rituals and ceremonies being performed throughout the day, making it a significant religious and cultural landmark in Kolkata.
